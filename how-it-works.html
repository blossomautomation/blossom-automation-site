CRM a mess?

How it works

A real process.
Not a sales pitch.

Three phases. Every engagement runs through all of them. What changes is how long we spend in each one.

The framework

Dump. Sort. Decide.

Three words. Enough to run every CRM engagement we've ever done.

1

Dump

Understand what exists. No judgment.

Before anything gets fixed, we need to see it clearly. That means going through what's actually in your CRM today, not what's supposed to be there. Pipelines. Properties. Workflows. Contact records. All of it.

Most teams have never done this. They've added to the system for years without stopping to look at what accumulated. This phase does that.

2

Sort

Separate signal from noise.

Not everything we find in Dump needs to be fixed. Some of it just needs to be documented. Some of it can be deleted. The Sort phase is about making those calls before touching anything.

The goal is a short list of things that actually matter, in the order they should be addressed. Not a backlog of 40 improvements with no prioritization.

3

Decide

Move with clarity. Not guesswork.

The Decide phase is where work happens: fixing, building, documenting, training. But it only works because Dump and Sort came first. Every change we make in this phase traces back to something specific we found.

That's what makes this feel different. You're not just getting changes. You're getting changes you can explain to your team.

In practice

What actually happens in each phase.

The framework is simple. The work inside it is specific.

Phase 01

Dump

We go through everything. You don't have to prep.

The Dump phase starts with a kickoff call where we ask you to describe your process, not your system. How does a lead become a customer? Who touches it along the way? What happens when something falls through the cracks?

From there, we get into the portal. We document what's there: every pipeline stage, every active workflow, every custom property, every list that someone created and nobody uses anymore. We're not evaluating yet. We're just recording.

This usually takes one to two weeks, depending on how complex the portal is. You'll hear from us when we have questions. Otherwise we're quiet and working.

What this looks like for a typical client

A sales manager at a 30-person company. Three people have admin access to HubSpot. None of them knows what the other two have built. The Dump phase produces a map of all of it for the first time.

Phase 02

Sort

We categorize before we fix. Always.

The Sort phase is where we take everything from Dump and put it into three buckets: keep it, fix it, or remove it. This sounds simple. It usually surfaces disagreements you didn't know you had.

A workflow that one person built three years ago. A pipeline stage that nobody uses but everyone is afraid to delete. A contact property that appears on every record but contains data in 12 different formats. Each one gets a decision, and we make that decision with you, not for you.

At the end of Sort, we have a short prioritized list. The top three items on that list are the ones we address first, in order. Not all at once.

What this looks like for a typical client

We find 11 deal stages across two pipelines. Six of them describe the same part of the process in different words. Sort produces a single pipeline with five stages, and a written explanation of what each one means.

Phase 03

Decide

We build what your team will actually use.

The Decide phase is execution. But it's not execution based on what we think is best. It's execution based on what Dump and Sort found, filtered through how your team actually works.

Every change we make gets documented as we go. Not in a technical spec nobody reads. In plain language, in the same place your team can find it, so the next person who touches this system understands why it looks the way it does.

This phase ends when your team can operate the system without us watching. That's the deliverable.

What this looks like for a typical client

We rebuild three automated workflows, write a one-page process guide for the sales team, and train two people who will own the system going forward. Three weeks of work. No open questions at the end of it.

The timeline

Week by week, what to expect.

Foundation Sprint runs four weeks. Here's how they're spent.

W1
Kickoff

Discovery call. We learn your process, not your system. You give us portal access and we start Dump.

W2
Dump complete

Full portal documented. Everything in writing. We share the raw findings before moving to Sort.

W3
Sort session

Working call with you to categorize the findings and agree on priorities. Decisions made together, not handed to you.

W4
Report delivered

Written gap analysis and prioritized action plan. The next three things to fix, in order, with context.

What you leave with

Concrete things. Not vague promises.

At the end of a Foundation Sprint, you have four specific deliverables. No dependency on us to use any of them.

Deliverable 01

Portal audit document

A written record of everything we found in your CRM. Every pipeline, stage, workflow, and property. What's active, what's dormant, what's missing. The baseline your team has never had.

Deliverable 02

Gap analysis report

Where your system diverges from how your team actually works. The specific places where data goes missing, processes break down, or automation fires on the wrong trigger. Named, not implied.

Deliverable 03

Prioritized action plan

The next three things to fix, in order. Each one described in plain language with enough context that you or someone else could execute it. Not a backlog. A sequence.

Deliverable 04

90-minute findings session

A working call to walk through everything we found. You can ask every question. We don't hand off a document and disappear. This call closes the sprint and opens the next decision.
