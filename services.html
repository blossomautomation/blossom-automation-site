Foundation Sprint

Start here. Every time.

You don't know what's broken. You know something is. This sprint names it.

Most organizations that reach out have the same problem in different clothes. Data they don't trust. Workflows that nobody follows. A CRM that technically works but practically doesn't. The Foundation Sprint is four weeks of structured diagnosis. We audit your portal, document what we find, and hand you a written report with the next three things to fix, in order. You leave with a decision, not a list of options.

This is not a discovery call stretched into a month. It is a real engagement with a real deliverable at the end.

  • Full portal audit. Every pipeline, property, workflow, and contact list reviewed and documented against what you told us your process is.
  • Gap analysis report. Written document identifying what's misaligned, what's abandoned, and what's actively costing you.
  • Prioritized action plan. The next three things to fix, in order, with enough context that someone else could execute them.
  • One working session. 90 minutes to walk through the findings and answer every question before we hand it off.
  • You own everything. The report, the findings, the plan. No dependency on Blossom to use what we build.

Not the right fit if

You already know exactly what's broken and you need someone to fix it. If you're past diagnosis and into execution, the Growth Retainer is the better starting point.

Growth Retainer

Someone watching the system. Every month.

The CRM doesn't break all at once. It drifts. The retainer catches the drift.

You're the de facto CRM owner on top of everything else you do. That means the system gets attention when something breaks badly enough to complain about, and ignored the rest of the time. The Growth Retainer puts someone in the system every month whose only job is to notice what you don't have time to notice.

This is not a block of hours you burn down. It's a relationship. We show up with a working session, run the maintenance your system needs, and flag the things that are quietly costing you.

  • Monthly working session. 60 to 90 minutes, agenda set by what the system needs that month.
  • Ongoing CRM maintenance. Property cleanup, workflow audits, pipeline hygiene, data deduplication as needed.
  • Async support. Questions answered within one business day. No ticket system, no queue.
  • Monthly status summary. Short written update on what changed, what we flagged, what's next.
  • Shared Notion workspace. Running log of decisions, open items, and what we've touched so nothing lives only in email.

Not the right fit if

You're still in diagnosis mode and not sure what needs to change. Start with the Foundation Sprint first. The retainer works best when there's a baseline we've both seen.

Embedded Partner

Not a vendor. Part of the team.

For teams where the CRM is touching every part of the business.

Some organizations are past the point where occasional check-ins are enough. The deals are moving faster. The team is bigger. The CRM is touching more parts of the business. At that point, a monthly retainer is the wrong shape. What you need is someone who operates inside your business on a weekly basis and knows your systems as well as you do.

The Embedded Partner engagement puts Blossom inside your operation. Weekly working sessions. Slack access during business hours. Real-time response when something breaks. Strategy involvement before decisions get made, not after. This is the engagement for companies that have outgrown piecemeal CRM support and need a true operational partner.

  • Weekly working sessions. Standing time on the calendar, agenda shaped by what's actually happening in the business that week.
  • Slack access during business hours. Direct line. Not a ticket, not an email thread.
  • Strategy involvement. Input before decisions get made, not cleanup after.
  • Full system ownership. We manage the CRM so your team doesn't have to think about it.
  • Quarterly business review. Structured review of what the data says, what changed, and what the next quarter looks like.

Not the right fit if

You're a smaller team that needs cleanup and occasional maintenance. The Growth Retainer is built for you. Embedded Partner is for organizations where the CRM complexity has outgrown a monthly check-in.
