About

We help small teams turn messy CRMs
into systems they actually trust.

A small consultancy. One owner. Honest pricing.

The practice

We do CRM support. That's the whole thing.

Most organizations that reach out have the same problem in different clothes. Data they don't trust. Workflows that nobody follows. A CRM that technically works but practically doesn't. We help them find what's actually broken, fix it in the right order, and build something their team will use without needing to become technical experts.

We work with HubSpot primarily, and with Salesforce, Pipedrive, and Zoho depending on where the client already is. We don't migrate people off platforms that are working. We make the platform they have work the way it was supposed to.

How we got here

We didn't come to CRM through a straight line. That's the reason we work the way we do.

Before CRMs, we managed a neighborhood bar in San Antonio called Tap Exchange. We were 24. Our stepfather owned it. We ran the floor.

One of our regulars was named David. Double whisky, neat. He came in most weeks. He was a widower, and he didn't talk much about why he was there, but we knew.

On the nights it ran well, someone behind the bar would have his drink ready before he sat down. On the nights it didn't, he'd order it like a stranger.

The bar wasn't really in the whisky business. It was in the business of remembering people. The whole job, on any given shift, was making sure the next person on the schedule knew what we knew.

We didn't have a word for that yet.

Years later, working in CRM, the same shape kept showing up. Different rooms. Same job. Make sure the next person on shift knows what the team already knows.

The team

One owner. Every engagement.

Oscar Gonzalez is the founder and lead consultant on every Blossom engagement. We've been HubSpot certified since 2016 and became a HubSpot Solutions Partner in 2024. We're based in Austin, Texas.

We work with small teams. Five to fifty people, already on HubSpot or Salesforce or Pipedrive, tired of patching the same problems. That's the situation we know how to help with.
